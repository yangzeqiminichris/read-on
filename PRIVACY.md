# ReadOn Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-28_

ReadOn is designed to keep your data on your own device.

## What ReadOn stores

To do its job, ReadOn saves the following in your browser's local extension
storage (`chrome.storage.local`):

- The pages you mark (URL and title), the scroll position and a short text anchor
  used to relocate that position.
- Any names, notes, tags, and aliases you add to your marks.

## What ReadOn does **not** do

- It does **not** send your data to any server. There is no backend, no account,
  and no analytics.
- It does **not** track your browsing, collect personal information, or share or
  sell any data.
- Data leaves your device only if **you** explicitly use the Export feature to
  save a JSON file.

## Permissions

- **storage** — save your marks, notes, tags, and aliases locally.
- **activeTab** / **scripting** — read and restore the scroll position of the
  page you are currently viewing, only when you click Mark or Jump.
- **tabs** — open a saved mark's page and navigate to the right URL when you jump
  to it from the All Marks list.
- Access to web pages (`http`/`https`) — required to capture and restore your
  reading position on the pages you choose to mark.

## Removing your data

Uninstalling the extension removes all locally stored data. You can also delete
individual marks at any time from the popup or the full manager.
